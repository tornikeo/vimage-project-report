VIMAGE

A story about optimization, applied ML and Google Cloud

Our story begins on one pleasant afternoon, with a zoom call from VIMAGE. In case you don't know what VIMAGE does: They made an app that is really good at "socializing the image editing". See for yourself below:

Turns out they are pretty good! A fair few customers, but there's a catch. They are bleeding money. The image pipeline they had introduced as a core service inside the app is taking way too long, churning up too much cloud resources, and is also quite frequently messes up background-foreground separation (this is useful for parallax). Worse still, most of the code, as expected of a former successful startup, looks like a main ingredient of bolognese - spaghetti.

That's the growing wounds VIMAGE is currently trying heal - in addition to a clear willingness to move most of the infrastructure to Google Cloud - why would you ever want to touch Google Cloud with anything but a 10 foot pole? If I had to guess, it was likely a bunch of credits that Google Cloud offered as a sweetener. Who knows?

So, it's set: Improve speed, accuracy, and move everything Google Cloud, as efficiently as possible. Ah, and also, integrate with the existing firebase authentication flow - very please.
